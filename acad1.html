ACADEMICS

Learning has its New Name...



Introduction

The academic system of IIT Bhilai has been derived from the system of our mentor institute, IIT Hyderabad. The system, called the Fractal System, involves distributing courses across semesters. The curriculum gives due importance to developing crucial practical skills required in industry, in addition to the required theoretical knowledge. Nearly all the classes are now being conducted by professors hired within the institute, with a few professors from IITH being present as well.


Fractal Academics

Before we dive right into the departmental subjects offered lets first talk about the common subjects that are taught to all the streams. Since you will be studying in the Fractal system, a drastically different system compared to the existing systems in place at the other IIT’s which follow mostly the traditional system; you will be introduced to the core subjects from the very first semester. The common subjects that we study are slightly different from what is taught elsewhere and is slightly more industry inclined. With that we begin the list of subjects.


  • What is fractal system?
  • Why fractal system?

What is Fractal System?

IIT Bhilai has adopted a very innovative academic structure (courtesy IIT Hyderabad), referred to as Fractal Academics which is the first step in creating an experiential learning ambience.

  • ○ At the centre of Fractal Academics is the concept of T-education -- Breadth and Depth.

  • ○ Under ‘T’ education, we have classified the courses that require width and length for training separately.

  • ○ Subsequently, each subject is fractalised (divided) into various segments with 1 or 2 credits.

  • ○ Breadth courses are basic building block for the depth courses.

  • ○ The fundamental idea is to atomize the credits to enable the students to get breadth in a large number of courses and depth in the courses of your choice.

  • ○ Initial emphasis is on breadth courses (first four semesters) and then on depth.

  • ○ Breadth courses are typically of 1 credit. There are plenty of such courses to help build a broad perspective. Introductory core courses provide students a window into further important topics.

  • ○ Depth courses are built on the foundation set by the breadth courses and are of 2 credits or more. For e.g., courses in the first semester are intended to provide a glimpse of all the core areas in EE viz., control systems, communication and signal processing, microelectronics and power electronics.

  • ○ In-depth coverage of core topics is offered in the depth courses.

  • ○ Often students build products/prototypes – bridging gulf between theory and practice.

  • ○ A balance is sought between technical and non-technical courses to reduce stress in students.

  • ○ There are numerous liberal arts/creative arts courses too depending upon student’s choice. (These include 1 credit courses related to Economics, Arts, Performing Arts, Management etc.)

Why Fractal System?

  • ○ Better access to a wide variety of courses increases exposure and preparedness for research. This fosters undergraduate R&D.

  • ○ Faculty is more of a mentor than a lecturer.

  • ○ Fractal academics enable a lot more flexibility in the academic program.

  • ○ There are departmental core courses in the very first semester and this creates an excitement to pursue research, develop new technologies and possibly be entrepreneurs.

  • ○ The breadth courses help students make an informed decision on their specialization choices.

  • ○  Students get exposure to basic tools early. Here: MatLab, SolidEdge, GNU Octave, Arduino Genuino and many more.

Some Pointers to Maximize the benefits of Fractal System

  • ○ The design of the system itself reduces the course burden, hence giving you ample amount of time to explore the topic.

  • ○ Observe what intrigues you, which topic catches your fancy and start working on it. In a straight forward fashion: Discover your passion.

  • ○ Many courses will be hands on (significant duration of course will be in labs or on computers), hence there will be ample opportunities to pick up practical skills which actually matter in the industry.

  • ○ The flexibility of courses will also give you more than enough time to pursue some non-academic interests also like music, sports, arts etc. We recommend you all to pick up at least one of them. After all, “Break toh banta hai Boss”

  • ○ A balance is sought between technical and non-technical courses to reduce stress in students.

  • ○ With increased flexibility, creeps in lethargy. It is always better to be alert and spend some time with each subject taught to avoid unwanted outcomes. (You know what we mean: P)

Computer Science & Engineering

About the Department: The first thing which pops up when you think of CSE is programming. Given that coding is an integral part of Computer Science, it is not the only part. As the name suggests, one learns both the science behind the computers and the engineering aspects of the field. CSE at IIT Bhilai is focussed on providing the students with a perfect blend of both – research and industrial facet of the computing world.

Courses during the first year: The courses for computer science students during the first year can be classified as follows:

  • 1. Department Core
    • a. Theory Course
    • b. Theory and Practical
  • 2. Basic Science Courses
  • 3. Electrical and Electronics Courses

The following sections collapse the bullets mentioned above:

1. Department Core

a. Theory Courses: For the first year students, Discrete Mathematics is the only theory course provided. The course is spread over two semesters with a weightage of 2 credits in each sem. It deals with the mathematical aspects of the computers. The part in the first semester will cover the following:

i. Boolean Algebra/ Logical Statements
ii. How to write proofs for various logical statements
iii. Modular Algebra
iv. Application of the above concepts in game theory, cryptography etc. at a very basic level.

b. Theory + Lab Courses: Introduction to Programming is the course taught during the first semester. The language to work with will be C. The course is taught from scratch and requires no prior knowledge of coding. However, one needs to back the theory taught in class with sufficient coding for the concepts to stick in your mind. Four or Five practical assignments with gradually increasing difficulty level will be given at regular intervals and the evaluation of the same will form a part of final grading.

The second semester will introduce you to the world of “Algorithms and Data Structures”. They are the building blocks of every software application you have ever fiddled with. Implementing the algorithms and data structures through a programming language is a skill which every computer science enthusiast should hone. The course is taught in C language and build up on the programming knowledge which you have developed in the first semester. At the end of the semester you get a chance to apply your skills in coding a project.


2. Basic Science Courses

a. Classical Physics (1st Sem): This course is common to both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. You have studied mechanics before, well this takes it beyond Newton’s laws of motion and introduces more powerful ways to analyse dynamical systems namely the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulation of Classical mechanics

b. Environmental Chemistry (1st Sem): Introduces you to the world of environmental engineering. You will study the basics of our ecosystem, atmosphere and how existing technologies can be modified to make them eco-friendlier. A glimpse into current water management and purification methods will also be given in the course.

c. Quantum Chemistry and Chemical Kinetics (2nd Sem): The course will have a very brief introduction to quantum chemistry followed by analysis of chemical kinetics (This part of the course will be all that you have learned during your JEE prep about chemical kinetics).

d. Electrodynamics: Builds on your JEE concepts of electrodynamics and goes a notch higher to explore the Maxwell’s equations and electromagnetic waves.

e. Linear Algebra: The concepts developed in this course will act as fundamentals when you take up advanced computing. The course primarily deals with matrices, builds up on the matrices you studied for JEE and takes you on a ride (pun intended :P) towards exploring advanced concepts such as eigen values and eigen vectors.


3. Electrical and Electronics Courses:

a. Digital System Design: This course is common to both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. It introduces you to Arduino and how the vast array of things that can be made using it. You will also be introduced to fundamental concepts used in Digital System like Boolean Algebra, Karnaugh Maps.

b. Applied Digital Logic Design: This course is common to both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. This course is similar to the previous one. It builds on it. It introduces you to Multiplexers, Flip Flops, Latches, State Machines. A piece of advice, it would benefit you to learn LATEX and Neso academy will be your best friend!!

c. Digital Signal Processing: You will be introduced to digital signals and how to process them. There will be a lot of theory assignments and coding assignments which will be mostly done in Python. So, learning a little bit of python would help you a lot.


Lab Facilities: The computer lab flaunts powerful systems (i7 cores) with both windows and linux distros installed in them. All systems have high speed net connectivity. The laboratory comprises of more than 40 desktops with all the necessary software and hardware installed.

Electrical Engineering

About the Department: The Department of Electrical Engineering, IIT Bhilai, since its inception in the year 2016 has been striving towards providing a vibrant atmosphere for students, in different aspects of Electrical Engineering. At present the department offers the undergraduate course to cater to the ever challenging needs of technical excellence in various facets of electrical engineering.

The Department offers Undergraduate program (B. Tech), Postgraduate programs (M.Tech.) and Doctorate (PhD) program from the session beginning in 2017. The research interests of our faculty encompass a wide gamut of sub-disciplines of Electrical Engineering. We have state-of- the-art lab facilities along with trained staff to handle the latest equipment. The Department has been playing an important role in instilling the avidity amongst students and encouraging research in the various sub-disciplines of Electrical Engineering. In the coming years we see ourselves as pioneers of Advanced Research and Development in our field.

Subjects offered in Freshman Year

First Semester:

Classical Physics: This course is common to both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. You have studied mechanics before, well this takes it beyond Newton’s laws of motion and introduces more powerful ways to analyse dynamical systems namely the Lagrangian and Hamiltonian formulation of Classical mechanics.

Digital System Design: This course is common to both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. It introduces you to Arduino and how the vast array of things that can be made using it. You will also be introduced to fundamental concepts used in Digital System like Boolean Algebra, Karnaugh Maps.

Applied Digital Logic Design: This course is common to both Computer Science and Electrical Engineering. This course is similar to the previous one. It builds on it. It introduces you to Multiplexers, Flip Flops, Latches, State Machines. A piece of advice, it would benefit you to learn LATEX and Neso academy will be your best friend!!

Electric Circuits: Similar to the one you studied for JEE. A few new concepts will be introduce like Thevenin’s theorem, Norton’s Theorem, Independent and Dependent source, mesh and nodal analysis.

Magnetic Circuits: Similar to electric circuits. The syllabus includes Coupled circuits, Transformers.

Signals and Communications: What is a signal? How to work with a signal? How to sample a signal? How to convert an analogue signal to digital and vice-versa? How a signal is processed to extract useful information? All these interesting thing are taught in this course.

Internet of Things: How does your television remote work? This course teaches you exactly that. This course introduces you to censors, how to interface a sensor with an Arduino. How to automate everyday things using a sensor, communication between machines wirelessly; this course teaches you all that.

Bioengineering: Introduces you to the field of Neuro-Science. This course teaches you about the brain, the different parts of the brain, how the central nervous system works.

Second Semester

Matrix Analysis: This course forms the bridge between geometry and algebra using the concepts of matrices. This course introduces the concepts of simultaneous equations, Vector Spaces, Linear Transformations, Orthogonality, Gram-Schmidt process.

Differential Equations: Introduces you to the different methods of solving 1st order differential equations, the different types of methods of solving, Exact and Inexact equations, variable separation, Linear differential equations. 2nd order differential equations, existence and solutions.

Data Analytics: The course goes over the basics of Data Analysis. Teaches you linear regression, fitting models to a data, hypothesis testing, time-series analysis. Probability distributions, random variables, Bayes Theorem.

Data Structures: Arrays, linked lists, Trees are taught and how to use them to store and retrieve data efficiently.

Basic Control Theory: Applications of controllers and control systems, basic building blocks of a control system, types of controllers, thumb rules for designing P/PI/PD/PID controllers, advantages of control system, stability analysis, time response analysis, frequency response analysis.

Analogue Electronics: A basic introduction to nonlinear circuits and how to analyse them. MOSFETS and the different transfer characteristics along with the different types of amplifiers built using it are taught.

Digital Signal Processing: Similar to the Signals and Communications course in the first semester the only difference is instead of working with analogue signals you will be introduced to digital signals and how to process them. There will be a lot of theory assignments and coding assignments which will be mostly done in Python. So, learning a little bit of python would help you a lot.

Semiconductor Fundamentals: What is a semiconductor, types of semiconductors, P-N junction diode, transistors, and their transfer characteristics will be introduced in this course.

Embedded Programming: This course teaches you how programming was done 60 years ago. Introduces you to the concepts of assembly language using AVR ATMEGA. Assembly languages are a very low level languages compared to the high level languages such as C and are closer to the computer hardware than the high level languages. You will be taught how to code in assembly and at the end of the course you will have to do a project using assembly language.

Free Elective: You can choose any course of your liking. Just ensure that the course you take is not in your own department’s list of compulsory courses.

LA/CA Elective: Same as the Liberal and Creative arts elective in the first semester.

Lab Facilities: Currently one electronics lab is fully functional. The lab is well equipped state of the art computers running both Windows and Linux. The lab is fully stocked with different kinds of resistors, capacitors, inductors, printed circuit boards, multimeters, oscilloscopes, Arduino kits, Raspberry Pi’s, soldering machines and a whole slew of transistors and Integrated circuits.

Expectations from the Department: The EE department at IIT Bhilai even though being relatively new, thrives to provide students a deep insight into the core concepts of the subject. It encompasses the areas of traditional teaching methods and latest developing technology. Many Universities and colleges have separate courses under Electronics and Communication (ECE), Electrical and Electronics (EEE), and Electrical Engineering (EE) each having a specialization in the respective field. However, our approach is to bring to all of this under one common roof so that students are able to master themselves in the subject of their desire.

Mechanical Engineering

About the Department: Have you ever wondered how rockets and flights are manoeuvred? Does the flourishing sound of an engine and manufacturing processes tingle your curiosity? Then this field is for you. You will get a boost to your mechanics, statics, solid and fluid dynamics knowledge and you will surely enjoy all of it, you’ll also learn how mechanical structures are supported, automation and robotics, workshops (e.g. welding, lathing) and their applications.


A Glimpse into Mechanical Engineering

Mechanical Engineering basically deals with the following domains:

  • ○  Design
  • ○  Production and operation of machinery
  • ○  Application of thermodynamics and mechanics
  • ○  Automated control systems
  • ○  Manufacturing

During the course you will learn and explore basic sciences behind the mechanical systems we see using the tools of physics. You will also learn to fabricate objects using different manufacturing process that includes moulding, 3D printing, Laser Cutting etc.

Courses Under Mechanical Engineering (for First Year B. Tech)

The courses can be categorised into following sections:

  • 1) Mechanics Related
  • 2) Thermodynamics
  • 3) Manufacturing
  • 4) Automation and Control Systems
  • 5) Mathematics
  • 6) Engineering Basics
  • 7) Chemistry
  • 8) Electrodynamics
  • 9) Biology
  • 10) Liberal and Creative Arts Electives

Mechanics related and Thermodynamics sections are basically an extension of what you have studied for your JEE preparation. Topics covered under this are Fluid Mechanics, Dynamics, Solid Mechanics, Stress-Strain, System of particles, Thermodynamics.

Manufacturing Section will contain theories on various kind of manufacturing processes from traditional workshops to automated milling, lathing laser cutting and 3D printing.

Automation and Control Systems will have theories on various automated controls using Arduino and Programmable Logic Controller(PLC).

Mathematics includes theory courses on Real Analysis, Sequences and Series, Calculus, Vector Calculus, Linear Algebra and Differential Equations.

Engineering Basics includes theory and practical approach of Engineering Drawing, Engineering Statics, Manufacturing and Fabrication.

Chemistry includes theory on Quantum Chemistry, Chemical Kinetics and Electrochemistry.

Electrodynamics has one course on Electromagnetism and Maxwell’s Equation.

Biology has one course on “Introduction to Life Sciences”.

Liberal and Cultural Arts Electives have courses apart from the departmental cores. Liberal Arts courses cover a wide spectrum which includes Economics, Financial Studies, Art and Culture studies, Management, Humanities, etc. Creative arts courses encapsulate courses on cinema, performing arts, historical heritage, etc.

LABS

In manufacturing section labs are for

  • ○  Workshop which includes welding, lathing, fitting and electrical maintenance.
  • ○  3-D Printing
  • ○  Do It Yourself (DIY) Labs, which includes CNC Machining, Moulding and Laser Cutting. The labs for Automation Control Systems are based on applications of Arduino and PLC.

The course content overall covers both applications and research sides of the department. If you are a true enthusiast, then you’ll love this course.

Expectations from this department

Having read the course contents, it’s obvious that Mechanical would remain in industry forever. So, after studying in this department one can also work in other domains of engineering such as Design, Manufacturing, Aeronautical and Thermal Engineering for research as well as practical applications. There are some really great opportunities for research in Manufacturing field due to need of mass production with less environmental side effects.
